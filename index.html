Private.
Not plastic.
Apple Pay is made to be private and secure. Plastic cards are vulnerable. They can be stolen, copied, skimmed, and even fraudulently swiped. Apple Pay is designed so that only you can make purchases. And you won’t have to worry about your sensitive card details being shared.
Learn more about Apple pay
Pay with a glance.
When you add your credit or debit card to Wallet, it’s immediately secured by the built-in technology of iPhone. Face ID is designed so that only you can authorize payments.
Learn how to use Apple pay
Private. Simple.
Protected.
Your physical card details are your business. And Apple Pay keeps those card details — card number, expiration date, and CVV number — hidden. Making a purchase shouldn’t expose your sensitive card details. It’s the privacy you expect with Apple.
Learn more about Apple Pay security
Designed with
security in mind.
There are multiple ways your personal data is protected. Apple Pay uses device-specific numbers and unique transaction codes to help keep your purchases secure. Apple doesn’t collect your purchase details and never stores your card number on your device or on Apple servers.
